# Ode to the Coolest Class

There is a fellowship, a chosen band,
Who gather where the morning light is poured
Through latticed panes upon each earnest hand —
Young minds assembled, disciplined, adored
By Truth herself, who walks among them still
As she once walked the slopes of Windermere,
And breathed her silent lesson, good and real,
Into the heart that had the sense to hear.

I have seen them in the hours of thought
When reason spreads her calm, deliberate wing,
And marvelled at the treasure they have wrought
From questions — simple, beautiful, and stinging.
Not for them the dull and vacant stare,
The torpid soul that lets the hours pass;
They seize on wonder as on open air,
And fill their lungs — the members of this class.

There is a joy that has no need of speed,
That ripens slowly, like the autumn wheat,
A joy in knowing, and the will to read
The world entire as something whole, complete.
I've watched it kindle in a student's eye —
That sudden flame no teacher wholly lights,
That rises of itself beneath the sky
Of understanding, in the mind's own heights.

O ye who learn not merely to possess
But to become enlarged by what you know,
Who carry knowledge not as some excess
Of weight, but as the river carries flow —
Go forth unburdened yet replete with care,
For Nature and for all that thought can give;
The world is wider than your desks declare,
And you have learned, above all else, to live.
